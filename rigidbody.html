Collision detection in game development

Learning objectives

At the end of this lesson you will:

  • Discuss why collision detection is crucial for game development
  • Describe the function of a rigidbody and the function of a collider

❗Objects affected by the physics engine are called Rigidbody Objects.

Take a look at this image. It may appear static because the ball is not being held by anything, it's just... there, floating in space. So, unless this is a game where gravity does not exist, or this is meant to be a floating ball, it should fall to the ground. It has no mass nor weight! It just seems unrealistic.

Objects are bound by physics. This concept does not mean that every game needs to have earthly physics, I have enjoyed 3D experiences where gravity is less than it is on Earth! However, there is always a set of rules, defined as the physics engine. For the sake of simplicity, let's consider the yellow circle is a ball on Earth, it should be affected by gravity, wind, and other objects. However, in the previous image we have a nonchalant circle without earthly worries like falling.

When creating a 3D environment, objects are not instantly bound by earthly worries but need to be added the properties. In Unity, we add a Rigidbody component to GameObjects. Objects with the Rigidbody component can receive forces and torque to move in a realistic way, influenced by gravity, other forces, and interacting with other objects.

This is now a ball with a Rigidbody component. The ball is indeed affected by gravity, therefore it falls on the ground and bounces. Even though it is just a yellow circle, adding a Rigidbody makes it look more like a real object, an actual bouncing ball.

However, it does not yet interact with the side wall, it just continues like there's nothing there, this is due to the lack of Colliders.

❗ To make objects interact with each other, we use Colliders.

Collision are components that must be added alongside the Rigidbody to allow objects to interact with each other. In the previous example, the ball does not collide with the wall because, even though it has the Rigidbody component, we still have not added Colliders, it simply passes the wall like it did not exist.

Now the ball has Rigidbody and Collider components. Now it interacts with the wall, it bounces off of it and moves to the left, as it would in real life. Again, it is a simple yellow circle, but the way it moves and interacts with the world and other objects makes it look more realistic, more natural. Imagine what you can achieve with more elements.

To finish this topic, think of a time when you saw a video or experienced a game that had something off with the physics. I don't mean like dream sequences with strange gravity or people flying. I mean times when you see a wimpy plant that can stand against a car going at full speed. What did you think about the game then?
